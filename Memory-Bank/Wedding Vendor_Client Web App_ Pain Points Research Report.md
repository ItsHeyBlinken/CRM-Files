# Wedding Vendor/Client Web App: Pain Points Research Report

## Introduction

This report summarizes research conducted on existing wedding vendor and client management platforms to identify common pain points, particularly from the client's perspective. The goal is to inform the development of a new two-sided web application that addresses these shortcomings, providing a seamless experience for both vendors and their clients.

## Research Methodology

Research was conducted by analyzing reviews and discussions related to popular wedding vendor management platforms such as Honeybook, 17hats, Tripleseat, Dubsado, and Aisle Planner. Sources included Reddit forums, Trustpilot reviews, and help center documentation. The focus was on identifying client-side usability issues, communication breakdowns, and general dissatisfaction.

## Identified Pain Points by Platform

### Honeybook

While generally praised for its ease of use for vendors, Honeybook presents several client-side challenges:

*   **Slow Performance:** Users reported slow application loading times, impacting the client experience.
*   **Email Glitches:** Emails sent through the platform sometimes experienced glitches, such as truncated text, leading to incomplete communication.
*   **Transaction Fees:** The presence of transaction fees was a point of dissatisfaction for some users.
*   **Legacy Features:** Changes to 
legacy features were not always well-received, with some preferring older, more user-friendly versions.
*   **Refund Delays:** One significant client friction point mentioned was refund delays, with reports of up to three weeks for processing [1].

### 17hats

17hats offers a functional client portal, but it is often described as lacking modern polish:

*   **Clunky and Outdated Interface:** The client portal's interface was frequently criticized for feeling clunky and outdated, impacting the overall user experience.
*   **Buggy Automation:** Automation features, while present, were sometimes buggy, leading to inconsistencies in client communication.
*   **Limited Mobile App:** The mobile application was noted as limited, which is a significant drawback for clients expecting mobile accessibility.
*   **Lack of Clarity and Visibility:** Clients expressed a desire for more clarity and visibility in their interactions, suggesting the platform can be overwhelming or less intuitive than desired.

### Tripleseat

Tripleseat's guest portal faces challenges primarily in communication and platform reliability:

*   **Email Deliverability Issues:** A major pain point is that emails sent via Tripleseat often land in clients' spam folders, leading to missed communications and frustration [2].
*   **Cumbersome Multi-Contract Handling:** Clients found it cumbersome to receive multiple separate emails for different contracts related to a single booking.
*   **Communication Fragmentation:** There were instances where clients had to reach out to vendors outside the platform due to difficulties in maintaining clear communication within Tripleseat.

### Dubsado

Dubsado is highly customizable, which can be a double-edged sword, particularly for the client experience:

*   **Steep Vendor Learning Curve:** The extensive customization options lead to a steep learning curve for vendors, which can indirectly affect the client experience if not set up optimally.
*   **Time-Consuming Setup:** Setting up the client portal to be effective and user-friendly is a time-consuming process for vendors.
*   **Suboptimal Mobile Experience:** Older versions of Dubsado were noted for lacking mobile-optimized features, making access from phones less ideal.

### Aisle Planner

Aisle Planner is effective for collaboration but can sometimes be perceived as overly complex for simpler tasks:

*   **Overkill for Simple Tasks:** For straightforward planning, the platform can feel like 
overkill, especially for clients who prefer simplicity.
*   **Perceived Downgrade from Simpler Tools:** If a vendor transitions from using simpler tools like Google Sheets to Aisle Planner, clients might perceive it as a downgrade if the new system is not significantly more efficient or user-friendly.

## Common Shared Pain Points to Address

Based on the analysis of existing platforms, several recurring pain points emerge that a new application should aim to resolve:

*   **Email Deliverability and Communication Reliability:** A critical issue across multiple platforms is the unreliability of email communication, with messages frequently ending up in spam folders. A new app must ensure high deliverability and provide clear in-app communication channels.
*   **Seamless Mobile Usability:** The expectation for clients to access all necessary information and functionalities from their mobile devices without needing to download a dedicated app is paramount. The app must be fully responsive and optimized for mobile web browsers.
*   **Information Accessibility and Clarity:** Clients consistently struggle to find essential information quickly, such as upcoming milestones, contracts, or invoices. The new app should prioritize intuitive navigation and clear presentation of information, allowing clients to find what they need within 30 seconds.
*   **Streamlined Payment Process:** The ability to pay invoices with minimal clicks (e.g., two clicks) is a highly desired feature. Existing systems often involve multiple steps or require clients to log into complex portals, creating friction.
*   **Reduced Communication Fragmentation:** Clients often resort to external communication methods (e.g., direct emails, phone calls) when in-app communication is cumbersome or ineffective. The new app should centralize all client-vendor communication efficiently.

## Conclusion

The research highlights a clear demand for a wedding vendor/client management platform that prioritizes the client experience. By focusing on intuitive mobile-first design, reliable communication, quick access to information, and simplified payment processes, the proposed two-sided web application can effectively address the significant pain points identified in current market offerings. This will lead to increased client satisfaction and reduced administrative burden for vendors.
